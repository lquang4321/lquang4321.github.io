Lithium-Ion Battery Bank

September 2019 - Present

A portable battery bank with adjustable direction and speed for manicure drill. The battery is in a 3S1P configuration to obtain a 11.1V nominal voltage. It is boosted to 17V via a boost converter to maintain a constant voltage. This 17V output voltage is varied via LM317 adjustable voltage regulator to vary the motor speed. The output to the drill is routed through a DPDT switch to allow direction switching. An LM324 Op-Amp utilized as a crude comparator is used to convert four battery voltage level into LED indication to display state of charge. A Zener diode is to ensure a constant 12V reference voltage for the non-inverting terminal of the LM324 as battery voltage drops.

The casing is 3D printed on a Prusa MK3S using PLA filament. It is comprised of three parts: front panel, box, and cover.
